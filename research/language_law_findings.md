# Verified B2B contract language law — all 11 CIS countries

## UZ — Uzbekistan
Russian permitted, no mandatory state-language requirement for B2B contracts between residents.
Source: gratanet.com "General rules for making a contract in Uzbekistan"; Закон «О государственном языке» (no B2B carve-out needed, law doesn't reach private contracts).

## KZ — Kazakhstan
LAW REQUIRES BILINGUAL: written transactions between Kazakh residents MUST be in Kazakh AND Russian (Статья 15, Закон РК «О языках» №151). With foreign party: Kazakh + language acceptable to both parties. Kazakh/Russian must prevail even if English also used (except Almaty AIFC participants: KZ/RU/EN all allowed). No invalidity penalty for non-compliance (dogovor24.kz), but the literal legal requirement is bilingual KZ+RU.
Source: adilet.zan.kz (official law text), dogovor24.kz, prg.kz.

## RU — Russia
Russian always permitted; no state-language mandate for B2B (53-ФЗ Art.3 consumer-language rule explicitly excludes B2B commercial activity between legal entities — garant.ru commentary).
Source: 53-ФЗ "О государственном языке РФ"; garant.ru.

## BY — Belarus
Both Belarusian and Russian are state languages; either fully acceptable for contracts between residents. Special bilingual rule only for specific contract types with foreign parties (construction/design contracts) — general B2B sales contracts unaffected.
Source: gratanet.com "General rules for making a contract in Belarus"; jurusluga.by.

## AZ — Azerbaijan
UNRESOLVED / CAUTION REQUIRED. Law (Закон №365-IIQ, Art.1.4) requires state language for делопроизводство of legal entities operating in Azerbaijan; active government policy limits Russian. No explicit B2B foreign-trade-contract carve-out found (unlike UZ/RU/BY/AM where carve-outs are explicit). Recommend conservative default: include Azerbaijani text, do not rely on Russian/English alone.

## AM — Armenia
No language restriction on private commercial contracts; any language permitted, prevailing language may be designated. Armenian only mandatory when a state body is a contracting party.
Source: gratanet.com "General rules for making a contract in Armenia"; GILS "Контрактное право: Армения" (two independent consistent sources).

## KG — Kyrgyzstan
UNRESOLVED. 2023 constitutional Закон «О государственном языке» lists mandatory-Kyrgyz domains including делопроизводство/документооборот, applying broadly to "предприятий, учреждений и организаций всех форм собственности" — but no explicit statement found on whether private B2B sales contracts specifically fall under this requirement (vs. internal recordkeeping). Recommend conservative default similar to Azerbaijan.

## TJ — Tajikistan
LAW EXCLUDES RUSSIAN: 2009 Закон «О государственном языке» №553 explicitly "полностью исключает использование русского языка в документообороте всех организаций и учреждений на территории РТ независимо от форм собственности" — applies to ALL legal entities regardless of ownership. Tajik required; Russian not safe to rely on alone for documents associated with Tajik legal entities.
Source: kdmid.ru (Russian MFA country brief); kumitaizabon.tj (official law text), Статья 1.

## MD — Moldova
Law explicitly states it does NOT regulate language use in private life (Ст.5, Закон №234/2020 «О функционировании языков»). No mandatory state-language requirement found for private B2B contracts; Russian permitted.
Source: legis.md (official law text).

## TM — Turkmenistan
GENUINE GAP — no explicit B2B contract language requirement found either way. gratanet.com's Turkmenistan contract-law guide covers registration/notarization rules but is silent on language. Recommend conservative default (include Russian + flag for manual legal review) rather than asserting safety.

## GE — Georgia
National-language priority case: Georgian is required to prevail; Russian/English alone is legally weak (a Russian-only contract is "just paper" for Georgian courts/state bodies per consistent legal-practice sources, requires notarized translation for state submission). Recommendation from Georgian legal practitioners: bilingual contract with Georgian as priority language.
Source: newsgeorgia.ge (JUST Advisors legal commentary, specific to business-language practice in Georgia).
